## 1. Duke Lacroix's fairy tale, from Philadelphia to facing Vinícius

Source URL: https://grenadiers2026.com/journal/duke-lacroix-philadelphie

Date: June 18, 2026

Category: Portrait · FIFA

Summary: On the eve of Haiti-Brazil, FIFA devotes a Team Feature to defender Duke Lacroix: raised in New Jersey, educated at the University of Pennsylvania, he returns to Philadelphia to challenge Vinícius Jr and the Brazilian stars.

Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jyu5HpdP1Os — Team Feature FIFA dedicated to Duke Lacroix.

As the long-awaited clash between Haiti and Brazil approaches at Philadelphia Stadium, attention is turning to a duel that resembles “David versus Goliath”. At the heart of this historical epic is Haitian defender Duke Lacroix, whose personal journey spectacularly intersects with the great history of his country's football.

### A homecoming full of emotion

For Duke Lacroix, this match in Philadelphia represents much more than an ordinary World Cup match. Raised in New Jersey, he spent four years at the University of Pennsylvania for his undergraduate studies. It was there that his football career took crucial steps. Coming back to this city to play a match of such international stature is a true childhood dream come true.

The player expresses with great pride what it means to wear the colors of his original homeland:

“It's definitely a moment I've dreamed of: playing against some of the icons of this sport, in the region where I grew up, with the country that I identify with and where my parents grew up. I couldn't have written a better script. »

The emotion is just as intense on his family's side. Lacroix fondly remembers the reaction of his parents during his first call-up, his “first cap”: while the national anthem sounded, his parents shouted with joy in front of the television and his father even cried. The announcement of his official call-up with the Grenadiers of Haiti filled them with joy.

### From the shadows to the spotlight

The journey of the Haitian national team in this 2026 World Cup campaign is the very definition of perseverance. Duke Lacroix recalls that at the very start of their adventure in 2024, the team played in front of barely a few hundred spectators in Barbados.

The contrast is striking: during their first group match against Scotland, which ended in a narrow 1-0 defeat, the team played in front of a huge crowd of 65,000 people. For the clash against Brazilian stars like Vinícius Júnior, the Philadelphia stadium will be sold out.

### Confidence and determination against the Brazilian giant

Despite the growing pressure and Brazil’s status as overwhelming favorite, the Haitian squad approaches this meeting without an inferiority complex. Lacroix emphasizes that there is a strong culture of self-confidence within the team. The players are convinced of their abilities and what they can accomplish on the pitch.

For Haiti, this match is the perfect opportunity to prove its value in the eyes of the whole world, Haitian and Brazilian supporters and all football fans. The stage is set for this great moment in history.

## 2. 2026 World Cup: Haiti is preparing to challenge the Brazilian giant, Sébastien Migné believes in it

Source URL: https://grenadiers2026.com/journal/migne-conference-bresil

Date: June 18, 2026

Category: Press conference · Pre-match

Summary: On the eve of Haiti-Brazil, coach Sébastien Migné responded to the press in Philadelphia. Cameroon's feat to repeat, pressure management, duel with Ancelotti, absence of Neymar: the main points from a dense press conference.

Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NJNLG47NJDs — Press conference by Sébastien Migné before Haiti-Brazil.

On the eve of a historic meeting for Haitian football against Brazil as part of the 2026 FIFA World Cup, the coach of the Grenadiers, Sébastien Migné, presented himself to journalists in Philadelphia. Between resilience, pressure management and memories of his past exploits, the French technician displays his ambitions.

### Reproducing Cameroon’s feat

Asked by a Brazilian journalist about his participation in Cameroon's coaching staff during the World Cup in Qatar, where the Indomitable Lions achieved the feat of beating the Seleção, Sébastien Migné shared the key to success:

“We simply have to duplicate what we did with Cameroon against Brazil. The hardest part at a high level is repeating high performances. »

The coach stressed that during this famous match in 2022, his star attackers (Vincent Aboubakar and Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting) had provided defensive efforts and exceptional runs. A message that he willingly passes on to his Haitian offensive sector: he will have to roll up their sleeves and run more than ever.

### Nothing to lose, everything to gain

For Haiti, which had not tasted the final tournament of a World Cup for 52 years (1974), facing the record holder for titles in the competition is a moment engraved forever. After a frustrating but encouraging defeat against Scotland, Migné insists that Haiti's place in this tournament is deserved.

Despite a particularly tough group (Brazil, Morocco, Scotland), the roadmap remains the same: aim for a historic qualification for the round of 16, in particular thanks to the format that allows the best third-placed teams to advance. The coach insists on resilience and is already planning for a third decisive match against Morocco.

### The challenge of leading from a distance and demystifying idols

Faced with the fervor of Haitian supporters, many of whom historically admire Brazilian football and stars like Vinícius Júnior, Migné's mission is also psychological: it is about demystifying the opponent while getting the best out of his 26 players.

The coach also spoke with emotion of the complexity of his role. Due to the difficult context Haiti is going through, it is impossible for him to reside there. If he relies heavily on modern technologies and video to follow his players playing abroad (like Jean-Ricner Bellegarde in Wolverhampton), he regrets not being able to detect more young talents directly on Haitian soil.

### A tactical duel against Carlo Ancelotti

This meeting will also be a strong moment on a personal level for Sébastien Migné, who will face one of the greatest coaches in the history of football, Carlo Ancelotti. Referring to his meeting with the Italian technician during the draw, Migné praised the “great class” of his counterpart while promising to be a valiant opponent.

Finally, when asked about the notable absence of Brazilian star Neymar for this meeting, the Haitian coach preferred to see it as a wink from destiny: four years ago, Neymar was also absent during Cameroon's victory. A positive sign to cling to in an attempt to write, tomorrow, the most beautiful page in the history of Haitian football.

## 3. The Grenadiers face Neymar's Brazil

Source URL: https://grenadiers2026.com/journal/grenadiers-bresil

Date: June 17, 2026

Category: Pre-match · Brazil

Summary: After Scotland, it's time for the Seleção. This Friday in Philadelphia, the Grenadiers challenge Neymar's Brazil, with the ban on losing if they want to keep their dream of the round of 16 alive.

The Grenadiers, beaten by Scotland last Saturday at Gillette Stadium for their return to the World Cup after more than five decades of absence, face Neymar Jr's Brazil this Friday, June 19 in a breakout match where defeat is rather inadvisable for the Haitians if they want to keep alive their dream of reaching the round of 16.

Scotland is a thing of the past. Now it's time for the Seleção, five-time world champions, who last lifted the prestigious trophy in 2002 in South Korea and Japan. It is this hungry team, thirsty for a title and somewhat frustrated after its draw (1-1) against Morocco when it entered the competition, that Sébastien Migné's protégés face this Friday in Philadelphia.

Fear in your stomach, trembling feet? Absolutely not. Head held high and absolute confidence!

Because beyond the gap in level which separates the two teams, the Grenadiers know that they will have behind them a whole people ready to dream of a historic exploit. And what could be more symbolic than doing it against a Brazilian team that occupies a special place in the hearts of Haitians?

For generations, the Seleção has arguably been the most popular foreign team in Haiti. From the streets of Port-au-Prince to the smallest towns in the country, yellow and green jerseys are part of the landscape during major international competitions. Pelé, Romário, Ronaldo, Ronaldinho, Kaká, Neymar: so many names that have fueled the passion of millions of Haitians for Brazilian football.

But on Friday, admiration will give way to competition. Haitian supporters will put aside their affection for the Seleção in order to wholeheartedly support the Grenadiers in what promises to be one of the greatest challenges in their recent history.

The statistics do not work in Haiti's favor. The last confrontation between the two teams ended in a Brazilian demonstration, the South Americans winning with a clear score of 7 goals to 1 (Copa America Centenario). A result which recalls the full scale of the task awaiting the Grenadiers.

However, World Cups have often been the scene of memorable surprises. In a match where the pressure will rest mainly on the shoulders of the Brazilians, forced to win to get back into the race for qualification, Haiti could try to play its card to the fullest: solidarity, tactical discipline and efficiency in front of goal. A finish which failed them against the Scots.

The Grenadiers will have the opportunity to write a new page in their history against one of the most prestigious nations in world football. The opportunity also for Nazon, Pierrot, Isidor and others to confirm why they are Grenadiers and at the same time shock the world by disgusting Brazil.

## 4. “Much more than sport”: the Haitian dream told by its players

Source URL: https://grenadiers2026.com/journal/national-anthems-haiti-documentaire

Date: June 14, 2026

Category: Report · Documentary

Summary: In the National Anthems documentary series, two Haitian internationals talk about what this return to the World Cup represents: pride, the diaspora, and a country that clings to its team. A film to watch.

Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JR6prnbl3pw — “Haiti's World Cup Dream Is Bigger Than Sports”, National Anthems series, on the MLS channel.

Some stories go beyond the field. National Anthems is a nine-episode documentary series, produced by Goodform Productions, which explores the identity and cultural DNA of the 2026 World Cup squads through the eyes of Major League Soccer players and their supporters. Launched in May 2026, available in short formats of around ten minutes published alongside the major tournament posters and broadcast in particular on Apple TV, Prime Video, Tubi, FOX and MLS platforms, the series set its camera on Haiti. The result is a dense film, where we hear directly the Grenadiers tell what it means to represent their country at this moment in history.

The film opens far from the stadiums, in the culture: Haitian pâté, the cuisine, the pride of a diaspora who wants to raise the image of Haiti at the moment when the team reaches the world stage. Because that is what it is all about, a first World Cup qualification in 52 years, for a country crossed by political instability, violence and ordeal. In this context, the qualification takes on the appearance of clarity: a reason to hope and rejoice, together.

The documentary recalls the scale of the challenge met. Unable to play any qualifying match at home, for security reasons, the Grenadiers played their “home” matches in Curaçao. And despite this handicap, they finished at the top of their group and won their ticket.

On screen, the voices of Danley Jean-Jacques (Philadelphia Union) and Derrick Etienne Jr. (Toronto FC) tell the same thing, from different angles: the pride of the jersey, the weight of history, the dream of doing better than all those who preceded them. Because Haiti has never won a single match in the World Cup, and this generation wants to be the one to change that, even against giants like Brazil.

Posted online by the MLS YouTube channel the day after the opening match, the film takes on particular significance. For their entry into the tournament, in Foxborough near Boston, where one of the largest Haitian communities in the United States lives, the Grenadiers found this boiling atmosphere of a real home match far from home, despite the defeat conceded against Scotland (1-0). The road now continues in Philadelphia against Brazil, then in Atlanta against Morocco.

The film says, better than numbers, why this team matters so much: it supports an entire country.

## 5. “We lost a battle, not the war”: Joseph and Providence refuse to give in to discouragement

Source URL: https://grenadiers2026.com/journal/lenny-joseph-ruben-providence-zone-mixte-ecosse

Date: June 13, 2026

Category: Mixed zone · Post-match

Summary: In the mixed zone after the defeat (1-0) against Scotland, Lenny Joseph and Ruben Providence praised the content of a controlled match, pointed out the lack of realism in the last meters, and already refused to give up before Brazil.

Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=56tHphNuNpA — The reactions of Lenny Joseph and Ruben Providence in the mixed zone.

Disappointment was visible on Haitian faces after the defeat against Scotland (1-0). But in the corridors of the stadium, neither Lenny Joseph nor Ruben Providence gave the impression of a defeated team. Frustrated by the result, the two internationals retained one conviction above all: Haiti demonstrated that it could compete with high-level opponents and remains fully in the running in this World Cup.

The overriding feeling was one of a missed opportunity. The Grenadiers held their own against the Scots for a long time, creating several dangerous situations without managing to shake the net. For Joseph, the difference came down to the final meters.

“We had a lot of action in the first half, after the goal too and even throughout the second half. We lacked a little precision in the last meters, finding the right person, the right space. We must be more precise and concretize our actions. »

Same observation on the side of Ruben Providence. The winger believes that the content produced by the Grenadiers must serve as a basis for the rest of the competition.

“We created a lot of chances. Unfortunately, we didn't manage to put the ball in the back of the net. But it is encouraging for the future. We had possession, we created situations, we will simply have to be more concrete in the last thirty meters. »

Beyond the score, the two players especially insisted on the quality of the collective performance. Long presented as one of the least experienced teams in the tournament, the Haitian national team showed that it could respond to the physical and tactical challenge imposed by Scotland.

Joseph even believes that this first release sent a message to the rest of the group.

“Some may have underestimated us a little. Today we showed that we could be dangerous against a great Scottish team. We were solid defensively, we knew how to put them in difficulty. These are very good signals for the future. »

Providence shares this analysis. According to him, the scenario of the match confirms Haiti's qualities more than it reveals its limitations.

“People thought it would be a lot harder for us. But we knew our qualities. We didn't think Scotland was going to walk all over us. On the contrary, we showed what we were capable of. Of course, given the way the match went, we would have liked to come away with at least a point. »

This confidence, however, does not erase the disappointment of the result. Both players recognize that starting the competition with a defeat was not the hoped-for scenario.

“Of course it affects us,” Providence admits. “We would have liked to start with points. But it's not over. There are two games left. Even against very big teams, we can still get a result. »

Because now, eyes are turning to Brazil and Morocco, two opponents that many consider to be even more formidable than Scotland. A prospect which, however, does not seem to undermine the ambition of the Haitian locker room.

“Today we surprised a lot of people,” Providence said. “We can do the same thing against Brazil or Morocco. There is no reason to give up. »

The same state of mind animates Lenny Joseph, who launched one of the most striking messages of the evening aimed at Haitian supporters.

“We lost a battle, but not the war. We will give everything to try to take as many points as possible until the end of the competition. Today, we saw that we could put the teams in danger. »

The two players also wanted to thank the thousands of supporters present in the Boston stands. In a context where Haiti still cannot host its international matches at home, their presence was felt as a real advantage.

“We need them,” Joseph insisted. “It’s very important that they make noise in the stadiums and push us during the matches. »

Providence now hopes to find the same fervor during the next meeting in Philadelphia.

“There were a lot of fans today. This is exactly what we need. This gives us strength on the pitch and helps us continue to give our all. »

For the two Grenadiers, this World Cup already represents a historic moment. Fifty-two years after Haiti's last participation, hearing the national anthem ring out before kick-off provoked a particular emotion.

“It’s something incredible to represent Haiti in this competition,” says Joseph. “During the anthems, we get chills. We want to give everything to make our people proud. »

A feeling shared by Providence.

“Hearing the anthem, seeing the whole audience singing, it gives a lot of emotion. This pushes us to fight and give everything on the pitch. »

The result therefore did not change the group's state of mind. In the Haitian locker room, the frustration is very real. But the conviction is just as strong: despite this inaugural defeat, the Grenadiers continue to believe that their history in this World Cup is far from over.

## 34. Wilson Isidor: a choice of the heart and history

Source URL: https://grenadiers2026.com/journal/wilson-isidor-choix-du-coeur

Date: March 18, 2026

Category: Portrait · Interview

Summary: For a footballer with several nationalities, the choice of a national team often goes beyond the simple sporting framework. It is an intimate decision, linked to family history, identity and the heritage that we wish to pass on. For Sunderland AFC striker Wilson Isidor, answering Haiti's call represents exactly that: a return to his roots and a commitment to an entire people.

In an interview with Sunderland AFC, the Franco-Haitian striker spoke with emotion about the reasons which pushed him to choose the Grenadiers, about the pride in representing his origins and about his ambitions as the 2026 World Cup approaches.

### A deeply personal decision

Born in France, Wilson Isidor has strong family ties to Haiti. Both his father and grandfather were born in the country. This dual membership has long fueled complex reflection regarding its international future.

For him, choosing between France and Haiti was not a simple career question.

“Sometimes making a choice is difficult. It was like having to choose between my mother and my father,” he explained.

Words that illustrate the full emotional charge of this decision. Ultimately, it was the desire to contribute to the development of Haitian football and to pay homage to its origins that tipped the scales.

By accepting his call-up to the Grenadiers, Isidor also writes his name in the history of Sunderland. He becomes the first Haitian international to represent the English club.

### More than a jersey: bringing joy to a whole people

The timing of joining the Haitian national team is no small thing. Haiti is preparing to experience one of the most important chapters in its sporting history with its participation in the 2026 World Cup, more than fifty years after its only appearance at the World Cup in 1974.

For Isidor, this historic qualification played a major role in his decision.

But beyond football, the attacker is aware of the symbolic significance of this event for millions of Haitians, both in the country and in the diaspora.

“I hope that with this competition we will build something for the future of our country and help many people who still live there. Bring them joy, a little freedom, even if it's only for a few hours or a few days. »

In a national context often marked by difficulties, football becomes a rare space of gathering and hope. Isidor perfectly understands this role and wishes to contribute to offering these moments of collective happiness.

### In the footsteps of Emmanuel Sanon

At the 2026 World Cup, Haiti will face a colossal challenge in a group including Brazil, Morocco and Scotland.

However, Wilson Isidor does not hide his ambitions.

For more than fifty years, a name has remained engraved in the history of Haitian football: that of Emmanuel Sanon. In West Germany in 1974, Sanon scored Haiti's only goals in the World Cup and ended Italian goalkeeper Dino Zoff's impressive unbeaten run.

Even today, his feat remains one of the most legendary moments in Haitian sport.

Wilson Isidor now hopes to write his own chapter.

“The first player to score is a huge legend. I hope to be the second. »

A simple but meaningful statement. Scoring for Haiti in the World Cup would not only be a personal accomplishment, but also a way to make the country's history.

### A new generation looking to the future

Before planning for the World Cup, Isidor will have the opportunity to fully discover the environment of the Grenadiers during international meetings against Tunisia and Iceland.

He says he is particularly impatient to meet Haitian fans, whose presence is particularly strong in the United States and Canada, two countries which will host a large part of the World Cup matches.

After realizing his dream of playing in the English Premier League, the striker now sees further. His goal is to build a lasting legacy, for his family, for his children and for an entire people who dream of seeing Haiti shine on the biggest stage of world football.

Through his choice, Wilson Isidor reminds us that football is not only a question of results or trophies. It is also a story of roots, identity and pride.

And for him, this story is now written in blue and red.

## 35. Ricardo Adé: “Facing Brazil is a dream come true”

Source URL: https://grenadiers2026.com/journal/ricardo-ade-portrait

Date: January 12, 2026

Category: Interview · FIFA

Summary: Captain of Haiti and central defender of Liga de Quito, the child of Saint-Marc evokes the return of the Grenadiers to the World Cup, his journey off the beaten track, and the intact memory of the great nations he once faced on the field.

“One of the characteristics of a Haitian is knowing that life itself is a struggle. It was never easy. We keep the hope that things will change and we fight for it. » Ricardo Adé, captain and central defender of the national team, opens his interview with FIFA.com with a formula which sums up his own trajectory as much as that of the team he wears on his armband.

The Grenadiers played their entire qualifying campaign away from home, their home matches being held in Curaçao. Fifty-two years after Munich, they returned among the qualified nations. “We fought the entire campaign far from home, but we felt the positive energy of our people. It will be the same thing at the World Cup. It is a great responsibility, which we carry with affection and respect,” he explains.

Group C – Brazil, Morocco, Scotland – is among the toughest in the competition. Adé does not underestimate any opponent, but does not present himself as a victim either. “The most important thing was to get to the World Cup. Once there, any group is difficult. We are in a very demanding group, but we have the means to do harm. It's a process. We go step by step. »

His own journey bears the marks of the same patience. Born in Saint-Marc on May 21, 1990, trained in Baltimore SC, he expatriated early: an opportunity in Asia which never materialized; a stint with a fourth division club in the United States; a return to Don Bosco; and finally, at twenty-six, his first professional contract — in Santiago Morning, Chile. The rest brings him to Mushuc Runa, to Aucas (the club's first title in seventy-seven years), and since 2023 to Liga de Quito.

“When I arrived in Ecuador, I felt like I had found my place in the world, my second home. I am far from my family, I work for them and for myself. I found my place. »

Among the three opponents in the group, Brazil occupies a special place in his memory. In 2004, he was fourteen years old when the Seleção, then led by Ronaldinho, Ronaldo and Roberto Carlos, traveled to Port-au-Prince to play what will go down in history as the “Peace Match”. A match lost 6-0, but an intact memory. “I was still a child, but I see this match very clearly on television. We are big fans of Brazil, Argentina… When these countries play, it's a party. People take to the streets. »

“My dream was to one day be on the pitch, to share this pitch with players who had already made a name for themselves, who were playing in the biggest championships. And maybe I will have the opportunity to live that dream this year against a team like Brazil. It's incredible. I can't put it into words because it's so exhilarating. »

He has already had this thrill once - facing another of the greatest. In 2018, at La Bombonera, Lionel Messi delighted Buenos Aires with a hat-trick and an assist during an Argentina-Haiti friendly (4-0). “Playing at this level, with so many people, with Messi and Agüero on the pitch, is like being in a dream. I keep the image. There's a photo that went around, a teammate and me on the ground — but it's Messi, that's what he's doing. It was fantastic to be able to be there. »

A question, almost for fun: which player, outside Group C, would he like to meet? “Cristiano Ronaldo. He's my idol, a player I really admire. His story speaks for itself. He works a lot, and I'm like him. »

On June 13 at Gillette Stadium, Haiti will face Scotland. It will be, for Adé, more than a match. “When the anthem rings, it will be the whole country. Not a single person less. Just singing the anthem on the pitch already gives me chills. Imagine in the World Cup. »

And one last wish, formulated as obvious: “My dream would be to be able, like other countries, to play a match at home before the World Cup, and to celebrate. Why not, fifty-two years later? I know the whole country will be with us. We know what we represent, and we are going to represent it well. »
