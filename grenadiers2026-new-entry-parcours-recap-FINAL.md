Pendant 52 ans, Haïti a attendu.

En 2026, une nouvelle génération a écrit son propre chapitre.

Cette Coupe du Monde n'a pas été seulement une histoire de trois matchs. Elle a été une histoire de fierté, de mémoire, d'unité et d'espoir. Elle a rappelé au monde que le football haïtien a sa place sur la plus grande scène.

## Match 1 : Haïti 0-1 Écosse

[VIDEO] https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TcCufmPCsu4

L'aventure a commencé face à l'Écosse.

Malgré la défaite, les Grenadiers ont montré de la discipline, du courage et une vraie envie de rivaliser. Haïti n'était pas là seulement pour participer. Haïti était là pour se battre.

Pour des millions d'Haïtiens, voir le drapeau national revenir en Coupe du Monde après 52 ans était déjà un moment historique.

## Match 2 : Brésil 3-0 Haïti

[VIDEO] https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lz3_atghfmY

Face au Brésil, quintuple champion du monde, Haïti a affronté l'un des plus grands défis possibles.

Le score a été difficile, mais l'expérience restera précieuse. Johny Placide a encore montré son importance, avec son leadership et ses arrêts face à la pression brésilienne.

Ce match a aussi montré le chemin qu'il reste à parcourir pour rejoindre l'élite mondiale. Mais chaque minute jouée contre le Brésil a servi de leçon pour l'avenir.

## Match 3 : Maroc 4-2 Haïti

[VIDEO] https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZU2ssk6Bmg8

Le dernier match restera gravé dans l'histoire.

Face à une grande équipe du Maroc, Haïti a refusé de sortir sans laisser sa marque.

Dès la 10e minute, Lenny Joseph a inscrit le premier but haïtien en Coupe du Monde depuis 52 ans. Un moment immense, chargé d'émotion, qui a rappelé l'héritage d'Emmanuel Sanon en 1974.

Puis Wilson Isidor a lui aussi marqué l'histoire avec une frappe magnifique de loin. Deux buts. Deux noms pour l'histoire. Deux moments que le peuple haïtien n'oubliera jamais.

Le Maroc a finalement gagné 4-2, mais Haïti est sortie la tête haute.

## Plus qu'une équipe, un symbole

Pendant toute la compétition, on a vu le bleu et rouge partout.

Dans les stades, dans les rues, sur les réseaux sociaux, dans la diaspora, en Haïti : le peuple haïtien s'est rassemblé autour des Grenadiers.

Des familles ont voyagé. Des supporters ont pris l'avion. Des enfants ont vu Haïti jouer une Coupe du Monde pour la première fois de leur vie.

Pendant quelques semaines, le football a uni tout un peuple.

## Merci aux Grenadiers

Haïti quitte la Coupe du Monde sans qualification pour le prochain tour.

Mais cette aventure restera historique.

Elle restera comme la Coupe du Monde qui a mis fin à 52 ans d'attente. Celle qui a offert à Haïti de nouveaux héros. Celle qui a redonné de l'espoir à toute une communauté.

Lenny Joseph. Wilson Isidor. Johny Placide. Les Grenadiers.

Vous avez fait rêver un peuple.

Et l'histoire continue.

For 52 years, Haiti waited.

Generations grew up hearing stories about 1974. They knew the name Emmanuel Sanon. They knew about the goal against Italy and the pride of seeing the Haitian flag on football's biggest stage. But for more than half a century, those memories belonged to history.

In 2026, a new generation wrote its own chapter.

This World Cup journey was about far more than three matches. It was about restoring belief. It was about reminding the world, and reminding ourselves, that Haitian football belongs among the game's biggest nations.

## Match 1: Haiti 0-1 Scotland

[VIDEO] https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TcCufmPCsu4

The World Cup adventure began against Scotland.

Despite the defeat, Haiti showed remarkable organization, discipline, and courage. The Grenadiers competed without fear, creating opportunities and proving they were not in the tournament simply to participate. They came to compete.

Millions of Haitians around the world experienced something they had waited a lifetime to see: their country back on football's biggest stage.

The final score did not reflect the emotions of the day. It marked the beginning of a new era.

## Match 2: Brazil 3-0 Haiti

[VIDEO] https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lz3_atghfmY

Facing five-time world champions Brazil was the ultimate test.

The result was difficult, but the experience was invaluable.

Johny Placide once again demonstrated why he has become one of the greatest goalkeepers in Haitian football history, making several outstanding saves despite Brazil's relentless pressure.

The match also highlighted the gap that still exists between Haiti and the global elite. But more importantly, it showed the direction Haitian football must continue to pursue.

No one expected the journey to be easy.

Every minute played against Brazil became another lesson for a young squad that now knows what the highest level truly demands.

## Match 3: Morocco 4-2 Haiti

[VIDEO] https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZU2ssk6Bmg8

If the first two matches reminded the world that Haiti had returned, the final match made history.

Against a Moroccan side ranked among the world's best and fresh off its historic run in recent World Cup history, Haiti refused to leave quietly.

Only ten minutes into the match, Lenny Joseph etched his name into Haitian football history by scoring Haiti's first World Cup goal in 52 years. It was a moment generations had been waiting for, bringing back memories of Emmanuel Sanon's legendary strike in 1974.

Then came another unforgettable moment.

Wilson Isidor unleashed a spectacular strike from outside the penalty area, restoring Haiti's lead and becoming another historic goalscorer for the Grenadiers. Two goals. Two new heroes. One unforgettable evening in Atlanta.

Morocco ultimately came back to win 4-2, but for Haiti, the scoreboard did not define the night.

The Grenadiers left the tournament with their heads held high after proving they could challenge one of the strongest teams in the competition, inspiring thousands of supporters inside Mercedes-Benz Stadium and millions watching across Haiti and the diaspora.

## More Than Football

Throughout the tournament, one image repeated itself everywhere.

Blue. Red. The Haitian flag.

From Boston to Philadelphia to Atlanta, Haitian supporters traveled from every corner of the United States and beyond.

Families drove for hours. Fans flew across the country. Children who had never seen Haiti play in a World Cup finally experienced what their parents and grandparents had dreamed about for decades.

For a few unforgettable weeks, football united Haitians everywhere.

Social media exploded with messages of encouragement. Artists, journalists, former players, influencers, businesses, and ordinary supporters all rallied behind one team.

The Grenadiers became more than a football team. They became a symbol of hope.

## A Farewell to Remember

The tournament also marked the final World Cup appearance of captain Johny Placide.

For more than a decade, he carried Haiti through qualifying campaigns, Gold Cups, Nations League matches, and finally back to the FIFA World Cup.

His leadership, professionalism, and resilience helped define an entire generation of Haitian football.

Few players have represented the blue and red with greater consistency or pride.

## This Is Only the Beginning

Haiti leaves the 2026 FIFA World Cup without advancing to the knockout stage.

But that is not how this tournament will be remembered.

It will be remembered as the World Cup that ended a 52-year wait. The World Cup that introduced new stars like Lenny Joseph and Wilson Isidor. The World Cup that united a global Haitian community around one dream.

Most importantly, it reminded everyone that Haitian football has a future worth believing in.

History was not only remembered. History was written.

And somewhere, the next generation of Grenadiers watched these three matches and began dreaming about 2030.
